# STM32 Wiring Guide (Step by Step) — Discrete-Module Self-Balancing Cart

English — see [中文](WIRING.md) for the Chinese version.

> This build uses one generic **STM32F103C8T6 "Blue Pill"**, plus the chassis's own
> **TK-TB6612-MD220A driver/power board** + **MPU6050 (GY-521) + JiangXie SSD1306 I²C OLED + HC-05 Bluetooth + HC-SR04 ultrasonic**.
> The balance control runs **inside the MPU6050 data-ready interrupt (PA5 → EXTI9_5, falling edge, 100 Hz)**, and attitude is solved by the vendor board's on-board **DMP** (quaternion → pitch/yaw).
>
> ⚠️ This document follows the **pins actually compiled into the firmware** (`Driver/*.c`). The old OpenCTR / RCT6 wiring is obsolete — do not follow it.
>
> 🔧 **The encoder pins are remapped onto 5 V-tolerant pins** (PA15/PB3/PB4/PB5), so the 5 V encoder signals coming out of the MD220A **can be wired straight in — no level shifter needed**.

## 1. Power Architecture — the MD220A is the Power Hub

**The battery connects to exactly one place: the MD220A.** The MD220A has its own buck circuitry: input 5.5–15 V, on-board **RT8289 → 5 V/5 A** and **RT9013 → 3.3 V/500 mA**, and it brings **5V / 3V3 / GND** out on its "**MCU interface**" header to feed the STM32 and every module. So you **do not** need to add any external regulator.

```
Battery (~6–7.4 V, 2S Li-ion)
   │
   └─► MD220A power input (J1: 2-pin terminal / DC jack, 5.5–15 V)
          │   ⚠️ This input voltage = motor VM. MC130 is rated ~6 V, do NOT put 12 V on it! (see §Caution 5)
          │
          ├─► TB6612 ──► Motor A / Motor B          (VM = input voltage)
          ├─► RT8289 buck ──► 5 V / 5 A
          └─► RT9013 LDO  ──► 3.3 V / 500 mA
                 │
   The MD220A "MCU interface" header brings the rails out → to the STM32 and modules:
          ├─► 5V  ──► C8T6 "5V" pin (on-board AMS1117 then makes 3.3 V core)
          ├─► 5V  ──► HC-SR04 VCC, HC-05 VCC
          ├─► 3V3 ──► MPU6050 VCC, OLED VCC
          └─► GND ──► C8T6 GND + every module GND (single-point star ground)

   Encoders: powered on-board by the MD220A through its "motor interface" (5 V); you do not
             power them separately. The firmware has remapped the encoder pins onto 5 V-tolerant
             pins, so the 5 V signals connect straight to the STM32 (see §Caution 1).
```

**Definition of "rails" (every "rail" in the tables below refers to these):**

| Rail | Source | What hangs off it |
|---|---|---|
| **VM (motor rail)** | MD220A input voltage (= what you feed into J1) | Only TB6612→motors; **= input voltage, use ~6–7.4 V** |
| **5 V rail** | MD220A `5V` (RT8289, 5 A) | C8T6 5V pin, HC-SR04, HC-05 |
| **3.3 V rail** | MD220A `3V3` (RT9013, 500 mA) | MPU6050, OLED |
| **Common ground** | MD220A `GND` = battery negative | All grounds must meet at this single point |

**Key points:**
- **Feed the C8T6 "5V" pin from the MD220A 5 V** (on-board LDO makes the 3.3 V core). **Do not also drive the `3V3` pin.**
- **The 3.3 V modules (MPU/OLED) take the MD220A 3V3 directly** (500 mA is plenty); don't borrow the Blue Pill's tiny LDO.
- **MC130 ~6 V**: the MD220A input voltage is the motor voltage, and full PWM in firmware opens it wide → **use ~6–7.4 V input, do not use 12 V** (see §Caution 5).
- **Common ground is mandatory** (see §Caution 6). Add a **100–470 µF** capacitor on the 5 V rail near the sensors to absorb motor-current ripple.

## 3. Step-by-Step Wiring (by module)

### Step 1: MD220A "MCU interface" header ↔ STM32 (the main harness — do this first)

The motor drive, the encoder signals, and the power for the STM32/modules **almost all come off this one MD220A header**. Match them up per the table below:

| MD220A MCU interface | To STM32 / module | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **5V** | C8T6 **5V pin** (also feeds HC-SR04 / HC-05) | 5 V/5 A supply |
| **3V3** | MPU6050 / OLED 3.3 V | 3.3 V/500 mA supply |
| **GND** | C8T6 GND + all module grounds | Common ground (single-point star) |
| **PWMA** | **PA8** | Left motor speed (TIM1_CH1) |
| **AIN1** | **PB12** | Left motor dir 1 |
| **AIN2** | **PB13** | Left motor dir 2 |
| **PWMB** | **PA11** | Right motor speed (TIM1_CH4) |
| **BIN1** | **PB14** | Right motor dir 1 |
| **BIN2** | **PB15** | Right motor dir 2 |
| **STBY** | **PA4** | High = enable, low = cut torque |
| **E1A** | **PA15** (TIM2_CH1 remap, **5 V direct**) | Encoder 1 phase A |
| **E1B** | **PB3** (TIM2_CH2 remap, **5 V direct**) | Encoder 1 phase B (same TIM2 as E1A, paired) |
| **E2A** | **PB4** (TIM3_CH1 remap, **5 V direct**) | Encoder 2 phase A |
| **E2B** | **PB5** (TIM3_CH2 remap, **5 V direct**) | Encoder 2 phase B (same TIM3 as E2A, paired) |
| **ADC** | Not connected | Chassis battery-divider sample port; this firmware doesn't read the battery, leave empty |

> The control signals (PWMA/AINx/PWMB/BINx/STBY) are 3.3 V outputs from the STM32 into the TB6612 (input ~2 V already reads high), so **drive them directly**. The four encoder lines are 5 V MD220A→STM32 signals, but **the firmware already remaps them onto 5 V-tolerant pins (PA15/PB3/PB4/PB5), so direct connect, no level shifter** (see §Caution 1).
> TB6612 truth table: `INx=1,0`→forward; `0,1`→reverse; `0,0`→coast; `1,1`→brake; duty sets speed.

### Step 2: Motors + battery to the MD220A

| MD220A | To | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Motor interface (XH2.54 6P) ×2** | The 6-conductor cable of each MC130 motor **plugs in directly** (motor power + Hall encoder are both in this one cable) | Don't swap left/right; encoders are powered on-board by the MD220A (5 V) |
| **Power input J1 (2-pin terminal / DC)** | Battery (~6–7.4 V) | ⚠️ This voltage = motor VM; MC130 is rated ~6 V, **do not use 12 V** (see §Caution 5) |

> Motor forward/reverse and left/right are **set at power-on bring-up** (see the checklist at the end): if a motor is reversed, swap that motor's AIN1/AIN2 (or BIN1/BIN2).

### Step 3: Encoders (already remapped to tolerant pins — direct connect, no level shifter)

The encoder signals are **5 V** on the MD220A (its on-board Hall supply is hard-wired to 5 V), but **the firmware remaps the encoder timers onto 5 V-tolerant pins**, so E1A/E1B/E2A/E2B go **straight from the MD220A "MCU interface" to the STM32** (wiring per the Step 1 table):

- Encoder 1 (TIM2): **E1A → PA15, E1B → PB3**
- Encoder 2 (TIM3): **E2A → PB4, E2B → PB5**

> **Key rule: both A/B phases of one encoder must land on the same TIM** — E1A+E1B together into TIM2 (PA15/PB3), E2A+E2B together into TIM3 (PB4/PB5). Don't split them or you'll read nothing.
> Which is "left/right" and which of A/B leads is decided at bring-up: if a wheel's speed feedback is reversed, swap that A/B pair; if left/right are swapped overall, swap the two pairs E1↔E2.
> (PA15/PB3/PB4 are pins freed by disabling JTAG; the firmware initializes the encoders only after JTAG is off, so they're usable; SWD is still kept.)

### Step 4: MPU6050 (GY-521, ⚠️ 3.3 V)

| GY-521 pin | To | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **VCC** | **MD220A 3V3** (3.3 V rail) | See §Caution 4, keeps the whole I²C/INT at 3.3 V |
| **GND** | **Common ground** | |
| **SCL** | **PB10** | Soft I²C clock (module has ~4.7k pull-ups to VCC) |
| **SDA** | **PB11** | Soft I²C data |
| **INT** | **PA5** | Data-ready interrupt → EXTI9_5 falling edge, **the 100 Hz balance loop is triggered by this** |
| **AD0** | Ground/float | Address = 0x68 |
| **XDA / XCL** | Not connected | Auxiliary I²C, unused in this project |

> SCL/SDA are on the FT pins PB10/PB11, but **the whole I²C+INT must be 3.3 V** — because INT runs on **the non-FT PA5**.

### Step 5: OLED (JiangXie SSD1306, I²C 4-pin)

The firmware drives the OLED power from GPIOs too: **PB7 high as VCC (~3.3 V), PB6 low as GND**, with soft I²C on **PB8(SCL)/PB9(SDA)**.

| OLED pin | To STM32 | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **GND** | **PB6** (driven low) / or common ground | See §Caution 3 |
| **VCC** | **PB7** (driven high ~3.3 V) / or MD220A 3V3 | See §Caution 3 |
| **SCL** | **PB8** | Soft I²C clock (open-drain) |
| **SDA** | **PB9** | Soft I²C data (open-drain) |

> ⚠️ Using PB7 as the power source has a current-limit risk (see §Caution 3). **More robust: VCC to MD220A 3V3, GND to common ground**, and leave PB6/PB7 unused. Must be the **I²C 4-pin display**.

### Step 6: HC-05 Bluetooth (for phone remote control, UART)

UART **must cross over**: module TXD → MCU RX, MCU TX → module RXD.

| HC-05 pin | To | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **VCC** | **MD220A 5V** (5 V rail) | Module has its own regulator, data lines are 3.3 V; firmware USART2 = **9600** (HC-05 default, no AT change needed) |
| **GND** | **Common ground** | |
| **TXD** | **PA3** (USART2_RX) | Module 3.3 V TX → MCU RX; a genuine HC-05 is 3.3 V so **direct connect** is OK; see §Caution |
| **RXD** | **PA2** (USART2_TX) | MCU 3.3 V TX → module RXD |
| **EN/KEY** | Not connected (normal data mode) | Only connect when entering AT config |
| **STATE** | Not connected | Connection-status output, unused in this project |

> ⚠️ PA3 is **non-FT and has zero margin**. A genuine HC-05's TXD is 3.3 V, which is safe; if you swap in some off-brand 5 V-TX module, **PA3 will be damaged** — before changing modules, measure the static TXD level (~3.3 V); if it's 5 V you must divide it down (1k in series + 2k to ground).

**Remote commands (single characters, case-insensitive; send from a Bluetooth serial app such as Serial Bluetooth Terminal). Commands are grouped below.**

*Driving:*

| Send | Action |
|---|---|
| `F` | Forward (+250 mm/s, latched until `S` or reverse) |
| `B` | Backward (latched until `S` or reverse) |
| `L` / `R` | Turn left / right (±60 °/s) |
| `S` | Stop (vx = vw = 0, still balancing) |
| `O` | Toggle ultrasonic avoidance mode |

*Loops / view:*

| Send | Action |
|---|---|
| `V` | Velocity loop on/off (velocity loop is **ON by default**) |
| `T` | Turn loop on/off (turn loop is **OFF by default**) |
| `Y` | Flip the velocity-feedback sign (default −1, "brake"; see brake test below) |
| `D` | Toggle the OLED between the **RUN** and **TUNE** views |
| `C` | Gated auto-midpoint capture (OFF by default) — finds the centre of a small steady oscillation and adopts it as the balance midpoint; heavily gated so it can't drift |

*Live tuning (select a parameter, then step it with `+` / `-`):*

| Send | Selects parameter | Step | Cap |
|---|---|---|---|
| `1` | Balance Kp | 50 | 9999 |
| `2` | Balance Kd | 5 | 999 |
| `3` | Velocity Kp | 200 | 20000 |
| `4` | Velocity Ki | 100 | 20000 |
| `5` | Motor dead-zone | 10 | 2000 |
| `+` / `-` | Step the selected parameter up/down | — | — |
| `M` | Capture the current angle as the balance midpoint | — | — |

*Save / erase to flash:*

| Send | Action |
|---|---|
| `W` | Save everything to flash (gains + midpoint + dead-zone + velocity sign), with a **read-back verify** |
| `Z` | Erase the saved config |

> Default pairing PIN `1234`/`0000`; map the M1–M9 macro buttons in the app to single letters and you have a remote panel. **There is no "disable balance" command** (that would just topple the cart) — to stop, send `S` (stops driving only, keeps balancing) or cut the power switch. Avoidance is off by default; after pressing `O`, an obstacle ahead at < 25 cm triggers: stop → scan in place → turn toward the most open direction → exit.

**Dead-zone compensation (`5`, default 120):** this adds the wheels' minimum-turn PWM to every non-zero command. That is what kills the slow static-friction limit-cycle wobble that Kp/Kd alone cannot remove.

**Velocity-sign brake test (the method for setting the sign):** in velocity-only mode, push a wheel by hand — the motor must **fight you (brake)** and channel 5 must oppose the push. If it *assists* the push instead, press `Y` to flip the sign; save with `W`.

**Default gains** (from `ax_robot.c`): balance Kp = 950, Kd = 50; velocity vKp = 4600, vKi = 4400; motor dead-zone = 120; turn Kp = 1000, Kd = 5000. Velocity loop ON by default; turn loop OFF by default; velocity sign default = −1 (brake).

### Step 7: HC-SR04 ultrasonic

| HC-SR04 pin | To | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **VCC** | **MD220A 5V** (5 V rail) | Module needs 5 V to emit reliably |
| **TRIG** | **PB0** | 3.3 V output trigger; HC-SR04 threshold ~2.5 V, 3.3 V is enough |
| **ECHO** | **PA12** | ⚠️ ECHO is a **5 V** pulse, but **PA12 is FT — direct connect, no divider!** See §Caution 2 |
| **GND** | **Common ground** | |

> **Don't swap TRIG and ECHO**: TRIG = PB0 (output, non-FT), ECHO = PA12 (input, FT). If reversed, the 5 V ECHO landing on the non-FT PB0 will cause problems.

### Step 8: LED / debug (optional)

| Signal | STM32 pin | Notes |
|---|---|---|

| Debug serial TX | **PA9** (USART1_TX) → USB-TTL RX | View `printf` logs |
| Debug serial RX | **PA10** (USART1_RX) → USB-TTL TX | PA10 is FT, a 5 V USB-TTL is safe |

## ⚠️ Cautions (check each one before powering on)

1. **The encoders already use 5 V-tolerant pins, so direct connect — but don't wire the wrong pins.** The MD220A supplies the Hall encoders with **5 V** (hard-wired on the board, can't be changed), so E1A/E1B/E2A/E2B are 5 V signals. The firmware deliberately remaps the encoder timers onto the **5 V-tolerant PA15/PB3/PB4/PB5**, so they can be wired directly with no level shifter. **The prerequisite is wiring per the table**: E1A→PA15, E1B→PB3, E2A→PB4, E2B→PB5. **Never wire them to the old default pins PA0/PA1/PA6/PA7 (those four are not 5 V-tolerant and will be destroyed)** — those four pins are now empty.

2. **HC-SR04 ECHO goes to PA12 (a 5 V-tolerant pin) — direct connect, no divider.** PA12 is FT, the firmware configures it as a floating input, and the 5 V echo connects fine. Note **PA12 is USB D+**, and most Blue Pills have a ~1.5k pull-up to 3.3 V on it — harmless to the HC-SR04's push-pull echo (which can drive high and low). We don't use USB, so PA12 is free for this. For extra margin you could add a 1k in series, but it isn't needed.

3. **Using PB6/PB7 GPIOs as the OLED power has a current-limit risk.** An STM32 pin is about 20 mA (25 mA absolute max), and a 0.96″ SSD1306 fully lit (charge pump) peaks at 10–20 mA — **right at the single-pin limit**, so it may sag slightly / look dim when fully white. **More robust: VCC to MD220A 3V3, GND to common ground**, and leave PB6/PB7 unused; keeping the displayed content sparse also reduces current.

4. **Power the MPU6050 from 3.3 V.** The GY-521 is labelled for both 3.3 V/5 V (on-board LDO), but **the on-board pull-ups and the INT reference are VCC**. Feed VCC 5 V and INT swings toward 5 V — fine for SCL/SDA on the FT pins PB10/PB11, **but INT is on the non-FT PA5 and would be destroyed**. So **MPU VCC goes to MD220A 3V3**.

5. **Do not put 12 V on the ~6 V MC130 motors!** The MD220A's **input voltage = motor VM**, and full PWM in firmware opens it wide. A 12 V input ≈ 2× overvoltage → overspeed, overheat, burned motor/gearbox. **Feed the MD220A ~6–7.4 V** (common 2S Li-ion); if you really must use a higher voltage, lower `MOTOR_PWM_MAX` in firmware so the average voltage ≈ 6 V, and retune the gains. (The MD220A/TB6612 themselves are rated to ~15 V — it's the motor that burns, not the board.)

6. **Common ground is mandatory (single-point star ground).** VM / 5 V / 3.3 V all come out of the MD220A, **but every module's GND (battery negative, MD220A GND, C8T6 GND, each module GND) must meet at the same point**. Miss one ground wire and the encoders/echo read garbage and the IMU jumps around. The motor return current (high di/dt PWM) should go on a thick ground — don't run it in one thin wire shared with the sensor/logic ground.

7. **BOOT0 must be jumpered to GND to run normally from Flash** (BOOT0 = 0 → boot from 0x08000000); only pull BOOT0 high when flashing via the serial bootloader. **SWD (PA13/PA14) is reserved** for download/debug and the firmware does not reuse it.

> Extra reminder: never accidentally route any non-FT pin (PA0/PA1/PA2/PA3/PA5/PA6/PA7, PB0, PC13) to the 5 V rail; among these, PA0/PA1/PA6/PA7 are now empty (the encoders moved away), so be extra careful not to casually connect 5 V to them. Before powering on, meter every VCC and signal static level one by one.

## Power-On and First Bring-Up (BRING-UP checklist)

Do these in order, **wheels off the ground first**:

- [ ] **Meter the rails**: at power-on, use a multimeter to confirm the MD220A 5 V ≈ 5 V, 3V3 ≈ 3.3 V, and the common ground is continuous.
- [ ] **Verify encoder pins**: E1A/E1B/E2A/E2B really go to PA15/PB3/PB4/PB5 (not PA0/PA1/PA6/PA7).
- [ ] **Test fall protection by tilting**: lay the cart over (large angle) and check that **fall protection cuts power above 45°** (motor torque cut).
- [ ] **Stand it up and nudge to auto-start**: hold it near upright and give it a light push — it should **start balancing automatically**.
- [ ] **Confirm the IMU's Y axis (pitch)**: fore/aft tilt must land on the pitch axis; if mounted off-axis, fix the MPU axis/sign.
- [ ] **If a motor direction is reversed, swap AIN/BIN**: if the cart drives off to one side by itself, a motor is reversed — swap that motor's AIN1/AIN2 (or BIN1/BIN2), or flip the firmware direction bit.
- [ ] **Encoder direction**: if a wheel's speed feedback is reversed, swap that A/B pair; if left/right are swapped, swap E1↔E2.
- [ ] **HC-05 pairing + remote**: pair the HC-05 from your phone (PIN 1234/0000), and use Serial Bluetooth Terminal to send `F/B/L/R/S` to test driving (USART2 = 9600). Try the tuning/save commands too (`1`–`5`, `+`/`-`, `W`); `D` switches the OLED between the RUN and TUNE views.
- [ ] **Avoidance**: send `O` to enable (OLED shows `AV:ON`); wave a hand < 25 cm in front and it should stop → scan in place → turn toward the more open direction. Send `O` again to disable.
- [ ] **OLED display**: there are **two views toggled by `D`** — the **RUN** view shows avoidance state / ultrasonic distance / drive Vx·Vw / angle, and the **TUNE** view shows the gains. If it's dark, first check PB6–PB9 and the supply (see §Caution 3).

> Always keep the wheels off the ground on the first power-on; with zero commands the motors should not move, the steering should be correct when tilted, and only then set it on the ground.

## Build & Flash

1. Open `Project/xproject.uvprojx` in **Keil µVision5**.
2. Select device **STM32F103CB** (requires the **Keil.STM32F1xx_DFP** device-support pack installed). Toolchain: **StdPeriph + FreeRTOS, Arm Compiler 5**.
3. **Project → Build (F7)** to compile.
4. Download via **ST-Link / J-Link over SWD (PA13/PA14)**.

> The firmware disables JTAG and keeps SWD, so download/debug is always available; using PA15/PB3/PB4 as encoders is a legal remap. After flashing, run the first bring-up (see the checklist above).
